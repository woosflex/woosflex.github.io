My Projects

AutophiGen: Automating Phylogenetic Analysis with One Command

Remember the endless command lines and software juggling during your final projects? Me too. That's why I built AutophiGen, a Python tool that takes the "phylo" out of "phylogeny" by automating the entire analysis pipeline.

So, What's the deal?

  • One-click wonder? More like streamlined workflow: Forget wrestling with software - AutophiGen takes your sequences and guides you through analysis with a single command. Easy-peasy lemon squeezy, right?
  • Say goodbye to repetitive manual work: AutophiGen aligns sequences, selects the best model, and builds your phylogenetic tree, freeing you to focus on actual analysis (and avoiding software wrestling matches).
  • Simple (not simplistic): Start with Muscle and Simple Phylogeny, but don't worry, more analysis options are coming soon (Maximum Likelihood, anyone?)
  • Visualization, FTW: See your beautiful (or not-so-beautiful) tree come to life with interactive graphics. You can export it for bragging rights, presentations or further analysis.
  • FASTA file fiesta: Throw multiple sequences in one file, and AutophiGen handles them like a champ. No more splitting and merging headaches.

But what's the catch?

  • Beta version blues: AutophiGen is still under construction, so it might not have all the bells and whistles just yet. But that's where you come in! Your feedback fuels my mission to make it even better.
  • Biopython Buddy: Blame it on my CS50p training, but Biopython's dependency for BLAST is currently on board. Don't worry, I'm working on finding something faster.

Why AutophiGen, though?

This project wasn't just about grades (although getting that certificate was sweet!). It was about learning, exploring, and making complex bioinformatics more accessible. Even with no downloads yet, AutophiGen taught me a ton about Python, algorithms, and user-friendly design. And I'm not stopping here - new features, performance boosts, and a smoother experience are all in the works.

So, want to join the Auto-Phylo-lution? Check out Autophigen on GitHub, play around, and let me know what you think! Your feedback fuels my mission to make bioinformatics less "phylo" and more "fun".
